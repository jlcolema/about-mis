MIS requires a smaller incision than traditional, open procedures. That translates to less pain. Patients are often less reliant on prescription medication and can end their use sooner after treatment.1

Patients are typically discharged from the hospital 1 to 3 days earlier following MIS vs. open surgery. In many cases, MIS patients may be discharged on the same day as the surgery or after one overnight stay.2

MIS lets you heal faster. Research has shown that, “....the minimally invasive approach is associated with significantly quicker recovery and reduced costs.”3

Positive outcomes and a faster return to normal activities have consistently been shown across a range of MIS procedures.4
CLOSEX
A smaller incision means MIS leaves much smaller scars than with “open” surgery.5
CLOSEX
MIS reduces the risk of bleeding and the need for blood transfusions. It also limits the chance internal organs are exposed to germs. That means fewer complications.6
